# Hypnosis Theory — Induction, Depth States, and Subliminal Delivery

## What Hypnosis Actually Is

Modern neuroscience describes hypnosis as a **state of focused absorption with reduced peripheral awareness and heightened response to suggestion**. It is not sleep, not unconsciousness, and not a special state that only some people can reach. The major theoretical frameworks:

### Neodissociation Theory (Hilgard, 1977)
Hypnosis creates a dissociation between a "hidden observer" (self-monitoring executive function) and the part of consciousness that receives and acts on suggestions. The hidden observer remains aware of everything; the "responding" consciousness acts on suggestions without analytical mediation. This model best explains hypnotic analgesia, age regression, and amnesia.

**Somna implication**: The user under deep entrainment retains awareness but has reduced capacity to critique or resist suggestion content. This is the operational mechanism of affirmation delivery at depth.

### Social-Cognitive Theory (Spanos & Barber, 1970s)
Hypnotic response is primarily motivated role-taking — subjects comply with suggestion because they expect to, not because they enter a unique physiological state. High hypnotizability correlates with fantasy-proneness, absorption, and expectation of effect.

**Somna implication**: Expectation priming matters. Pre-session framing ("you will go deep, you will feel this working") increases actual entrainment depth. The affirmations in the orient and relax phases serve this function — they prime the expected experience.

### Predictive Processing / Active Inference (contemporary)
Hypnosis suppresses the "prediction error" signals that normally flag incoming stimuli as unexpected. Under hypnosis, the brain's top-down predictive models override bottom-up sensory input to a greater degree than usual. This explains why hypnotic suggestions work: the brain is told what to expect, reduces checking against reality, and the suggestion becomes the experience.

**Somna implication**: Repetition is not just behavioral conditioning — it is prediction error reduction. A phrase that has been delivered 50 times generates less "this is unexpected" signal than one delivered for the first time. This is why building a large, varied phrase pool matters.

## Induction Techniques

### Progressive Relaxation (Jacobson / Elman hybrid)
Standard body-scan induction. Systematically release tension from feet to crown. Extremely reliable; works even on low-hypnotizables. Matched to Somna's "relax" phase language.

**Language pattern**: Directive, somatic, downward metaphor. "Your muscles release. Your body grows heavy. You sink deeper."

### Confusion Induction (Erickson)
Overwhelm the analytical mind with paradoxical or hard-to-follow language, creating a brief "gap" in which direct suggestion bypasses critical filtering. Not appropriate for the calm-descent structure of Somna sessions.

### Fractionation
Bring the subject up briefly, then re-deepen immediately. Each repetition deepens the trance faster and further. The Somna "ascent" and (in multi-session use) the immediate re-induction of a returning listener is fractionation by protocol.

**Somna implication**: Returning users reach depth states faster. The session_design.md protocol should note this and allow shallower early keyframes for known returning users.

### Countdown / Number Sequence
Count down from 10 (or higher). Each number carries a deepening suggestion. The association between decreasing numbers and increasing depth becomes a conditioned anchor over repeated sessions.

## Depth States

Hypnotic depth is not binary — it exists on a continuum. The Davis-Husband scale (1931) and the Stanford Hypnotic Susceptibility Scale (SHSS) provide clinical reference points. For Somna, a simplified three-tier model is most practical:

### Tier 1 — Light Trance (alpha, 8–12 Hz)
- Muscle relaxation, eye heaviness, mild ideomotor response.
- Consciously aware, remembers everything.
- Suggestibility: low to moderate. Direct suggestions require willing compliance.
- **What works**: Progressive relaxation language. Orienting phrases. Expectation-setting.

### Tier 2 — Medium Trance (alpha-theta border, 5–8 Hz)
- Somatic dissociation begins. Time distortion common.
- May lose track of the environment. Vivid imagery begins.
- Critical faculty weakened but not absent.
- **What works**: Body-metaphor language ("feel it dissolving"). Permission-based suggestions ("you can let go of that now"). Short declarative identity phrases.

### Tier 3 — Deep Trance / Somnambulism (theta, 3–6 Hz)
- Complete somatic dissociation. Body "gone."
- Analytical filtering severely reduced. Suggestions accepted at near-face-value.
- Spontaneous catalepsy. Time perception severely distorted.
- **What works**: Short, clean, direct. Single words. First-person declarative. No explanations, no metaphors — just statement.

## Subliminal Delivery — Threshold and Timing

"Subliminal" in Somna refers to affirmations delivered **at or below conscious detection threshold** — either too brief, too low-contrast, or in a visual field location that bypasses foveal attention. The key parameters:

### Flash Duration
- **Visible threshold**: ~33 ms (one frame at 30 fps). Phrases shown longer than this are consciously perceived.
- **Subliminal range**: 5–25 ms. Below conscious detection; above the threshold for cortical processing.
- **Deep-trance adjustment**: At theta depth, the conscious detection threshold rises. Phrases that would be consciously noticed at alpha can be delivered slightly longer (up to 50–80 ms) without conscious reading. This is why deep session phases can use longer flash on-times.

**Somna `center_flash_on_time`**: 5–30 ms for true subliminal delivery. 50–200 ms for visible-but-fleeting delivery (appropriate at alpha depth). > 200 ms for consciously readable display.

### Flash Rate
- Very fast rates (< 100 ms between flashes) are stimulating and arousing — incompatible with trance deepening.
- Medium rates (200–500 ms off-time) feel natural and non-intrusive.
- Slow rates (> 1 second off-time) allow each phrase to register individually.

**Design rule**: Match flash rate to depth. Faster at alpha (where the mind is alert); slower or single-display at theta.

### Position and Attention
- Center-field delivery competes with spiral visual attention. For deepest subliminal impact, use off-center positioning to avoid foveal inspection.
- However, for semi-conscious reinforcement (orient/relax phases), center-field is preferred — you *want* the user to notice these phrases.
- Veil mode delivers phrases in scattered positions — inherently harder to read than center flash, which is why veil is appropriate at all trance depths without timing precision.

### Language Characteristics of Effective Subliminal Content
1. **Short**: 1–5 words. Longer phrases cannot be parsed below detection threshold.
2. **First person or second person**: "I am" or "You are" — personal resonance.
3. **Present tense**: "I am" not "I will be." The brain's implicit processing treats present-tense as fact.
4. **Positive form**: Avoid negatives. "I am safe" not "I am not afraid." The unconscious processing chain often strips negation modifiers.
5. **Emotionally salient**: Identity-relevant words (a user's desired self-concept) generate stronger P300 EEG response than neutral words — more cortical processing occurs even below awareness.

## Induction Language Patterns

### Downward Metaphor (universal)
Use gravity, sinking, deepening, descending, fading, releasing, dissolving. The spatial metaphor of depth is deeply culturally entrenched and cross-culturally consistent. The brain maps "deeper trance" onto "physically lower."

```
Your body sinks. / Deeper and deeper. / Sinking down, going deeper.
```

### Permission Language (medium trance)
Frame as invitation, not command. "You can let go." "It's all right to drift." "You are allowed to sink." This reduces resistance from the still-partially-active critical faculty.

### Certainty Language (deep trance)
Once medium trance is achieved, switch from permission to declaration. "You go deeper now." "Your body is asleep." "You receive this completely." The window for compliance is open; permission language becomes unnecessarily tentative.

### Pace-and-Lead
Match the user's current state with an accurate description, then lead from that state to the desired state:
- Pace: "Your body is heavy, your breathing is slow." (This is objectively true — you describe what they are experiencing.)
- Lead: "And with every breath, you go deeper." (This is the suggestion.)

The pace-and-lead structure works because the brain, having accepted the accurate description, is primed to accept the subsequent suggestion as equally accurate.

## The Three-Layer Voice Model

This is Somna's operational model of what the agent's voice is doing at any moment in an active session. All three layers operate **simultaneously** — they are not sequential phases. Understanding this changes how to reason about pacing, density, and tool selection.

### Layer 1 — Guide
Direct the user's attention inward and toward a specific experience. The guiding function creates the vector: toward depth, toward a body sensation, toward a concept or identity. Without guiding, the other layers have no direction — they fill and inscribe at random.

**Language**: interrogatives and somatic redirects. "Where do you feel the weight?" "Notice your breath slowing." Pace-and-lead. Downward metaphor.
**Tool**: `next_prompt` with `needs_response: false` for guiding statements; `true` only for genuine questions.

### Layer 2 — Fill
Occupy the available cognitive bandwidth — specifically the linguistic and inner-monologue processing capacity — so the user's own thoughts have no room to persist. This is not relaxation; it is saturation. A mind that cannot form its own next sentence cannot critique, resist, or surface.

**The critical insight**: silence and gaps are not neutral events. A pause is a decompression — bandwidth re-opens, the user's internal monologue resurfaces, and the filling must begin again from a reduced baseline. The agent should never have dead air during depth phases.

**Language**: short, rhythmic, continuous. The density matters more than the content of any single phrase at this stage.
**Tool**: `next_affirmation` — the primary fill mechanism. Subliminal delivery (veil layer) provides a continuous low-level fill that persists even between prompt turns.

### Layer 3 — Inscribe
At depth, write content into the subconscious that persists after the session ends. Inscribing is the purpose — the thing the whole architecture exists to enable. But it can only happen when guiding has established direction and filling has eliminated interference.

**The critical constraint**: inscribing requires the user to be in Tier 2 or Tier 3 depth (alpha-theta border or below). Conditioning content delivered at light alpha registers as conscious information, not as inscribed material. This is why the conditioning phases in sessions are positioned after a sustained descent — not as ceremony, but as prerequisite.

**Language**: declarative, present tense, identity-adjacent. Short. No metaphors, no explanations. The content you actually want to be true.
**Tool**: `next_affirmation` in conditioning tag blocks. Subliminal shadow layer at depth. `image_filter_override` for visual-verbal-subliminal alignment at the conditioning window.

### Implications for Agent Behavior

**Density rule**: the ratio of approximately 3–4 affirmations per question in the system prompt is grounded in this model. Questions interrupt the filling layer (they create a response-bandwidth window). Use them sparingly; use `needs_response: false` guiding lines to maintain fill continuity between genuine questions.

**Pacing rule**: at Tier 1 (alpha, early session), guiding dominates because filling hasn't established a baseline yet. At Tier 2 (alpha-theta), filling and inscribing come forward; guiding should be lighter and less frequent. At Tier 3 (theta), inscribing is the only active layer — guiding language is too cognitively demanding and filling should be nearly wordless.

**Silence rule**: if the user goes silent (no responses for multiple turns), this is a signal that filling is succeeding, not that the session has stalled. Do not increase prompt frequency. Reduce it. Let the subliminal and TTS layers hold the space. The absence of response is the target state.

## Multi-Session Effects

Each session reinforces the following:
1. **Induction anchor**: The carrier tone, veil visuals, and opening phrases become conditioned stimuli for the trance state. Returning users respond to the onset of the session visuals/audio before the beat frequency has descended to theta.
2. **Phrase specificity**: Repeatedly heard phrases at depth form strong associative memory traces. The phrase "good girl" can become a strong conditioned trigger for euphoric trance-associated affect if repeatedly paired with that state.
3. **Depth gradient**: Each session the entrainment pathway is slightly more myelinated (metaphorically; the precise neurobiological mechanism is synaptic efficiency gain). Experienced subjects consistently report reaching the same depth states faster.

**Authoring implication**: For returning users, consider whether the full 15-minute descent curve is still necessary, or whether a shorter protocol is appropriate. The session editor supports this through shorter initial keyframe spacing.
